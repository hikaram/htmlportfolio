Beyond the Surface

Personal project, 2010, duration: 03:14.

 

Artist / Director: Egor Kraft

Camera: Ivan Apollonov
Production: Egor Kraft
Actor: Donatas Grudovic
Sound: Cosmic Honey

 

The video is a visual metaphor of existential freedom, predominance of essence over existence, and conditionality of boundaries it also has a sculptural dimension related to the evolution of form. The space as a black absolute presented as an allegory of authentic freedom as well as the narrative links to the discourse of how the consciousness and existence are related to each other.

The New Color

Intervention; video, installation; website; video duration: 2’39″; 2011.

website: www.thenewcolor.net

 

The project consists of a video, which exploits esthetics typically found in advertising, and also includes links to the project’s own website; both act to describe to us a brand new and previously non-existent color.
The project explores the media’s deliberate manipulation of imagery to make a product appear intriguing and desirable to us such as those found on a typical TV ad.
In today’s ever expanding range of human perception there is little that has the power to really excite us any longer — let alone appear as a completely new experience for human vision; here, we are going beyond the boundaries of the color spectrum. At the very beginning of the video a voice confidently states that: «Starting from now, our common concepts of color have become outdated…»
However, it is clearly apparent that this self-assurance and the various enticing effects used, which advertisements are often possessed of and that we eagerly succumb to, are deliberately and skillfully constructed to become for us a pseudo-reality – only seeming at first to be part of a world of pure experience.
The advert does not take part in the production of this new colour, but still plays an integral role, not only because it refers to this so-called new commodity as an object of consumption, but also because it becomes an object of consumption itself, the advert is in fact is the desirable object. It is necessary to clearly distinguish between its dual status: it is the discourse behind the thing, and the actual thing itself.
In the work «The New Color» the advertisement is placed within the territory of art — as an artistic gesture, and since the object being advertised does not exist and is most likely altogether impossible, all that remains appropriate to be considered is the work as an artistic subject.

Group exhibition «Insight & Foresight» at the Moscow's Center for
Contemporary Culture «Garage»

Artek

Mural at the gallery’s wall; graphite, acrylic; 2012;

St-Petersburg, Russia, «Artek».

In carrying out the realisation of the mural Karina Eybatova & Lesha Galkin  participated.

 

 

The name «Artek» is taken from a famous in post-Soviet states summer camp, located on the Crimean Peninsula.  On the basis of this fact and personal interest to such topic as reconsideration of the Post-Soviet territories in the context of the current situation emerged the theme of the artwork. Three stages of destruction of the bust of the pioneer, a typical soviet boy scout, is a metaphor for the collapse of the ambitions of the camp, that was supposed to be an international place of the national feuds, political differences and spiritual emptiness. Ambitions were dashed with the ambitions of the former Soviet Union. In fact in the third stage of the mural in the centre of the head is a round ball, like a core, and also there’re fragments of the crashed head that cast shadows on it, so that they form shapes of the continents of the earth on the core. This image symbolises the idea of cosmopolitanism as a means against political pressure, nationalism and involvement in the far-fetched processes.

State of Mine

Installation, object; 2700 badges; dimensions: 135×110 cm.

Courtesy of fond Ripley’s. Orlando, FL.

 

The project is titled State of Mine. It is a self-portrait that attempts to represent a vision of various information about the artist, his ‘information contents,’ such as the multiplicity of his thoughts, interests, declarations, key memories. I have created a set of graphics to illustrate all of these aspects, the character as a system of signs — a language to be used as a template for the creation of my own ‘information’ portrait, that could be used to describe me. As a result I have produced about 2,700 badges, with a range of around 200 unique symbols. Badges seemed a perfectly appropriate medium to use here, as they usually display a subject with which their owner identifies himself. I placed each symbol/badge so that its arrangement created my physical portrait alongside myself in terms of information. The works name also describes the idea itself, playing on the english expression “state of mind.” “State of mind” means a concrete condition as to where someone or something is at a specific time. A mental state is a kind of state or process only possessed by and unique to thinking and feeling beings. Such a definition of an object’s condition provides its conceptual contents, that creates a self-portrait where each badge and its arrangement visualises the author’s personality in the period of creation.

The House of Cards

Installation, dimensions variable; styrofoam, vinyl; 2008.

The large card house, consists of cards that symbolise aspects of social human life which can seem to be improbably important and vital, and actually can easily fail and loose a priority.  Like a huge something, built from wrongly placed priorities, can fail in any second as ambitions would fail, desires and vital principles of many people if war, for example, has suddenly begun or there would be a natural cataclysm.

Exhibited at the «Art Parade», Loft «Etagi», St. Petersburg, 2008.

Virtual Amplification

Single-channel video installation; video loop, 2’35″; 2011.

 

A question of vital importance to humanity is lost in ordered labyrinths of virtual space. Each new click of the mouse takes us further from issues like the way the development of mass media distracts us from understanding reality. Activity is transformed into a situational spectacle, in which the choice of a particular media as a means of understanding replaces our effort to perceive reality and the meaning of our existence in that reality.

Exhibited at Multimedia Art Museum Moscow

Semiotic Collapse

Site-specific installation; posters; 2012.

Shown at the goup show «Apocalypse and the Rebirth in the Chocolate House» at the Museum of Russian Art in Kiev, within parallel program bennale «Arsenale 2012″.

 

Poster texts:

 

1 The purpose of text is to deliver message.

2 Purposive message not delivered with text.

3 The deliverance of message is the purpose of text.

4 A text message on deliverance of purpose.

5 Textual message undelivered purposely.

 

 

Series of texts begins with the undeniable statement: «The purpose of text is to deliver message». Each of the following texts is the result of changing the order of words of the first text which results the situation where undeniable meaning of statement get totally deconstructed. This situation reveals the semiotic system and weakness of such medium as the text. There appear a new contradictory meanings, such as «Deliverance of message is the purpose of text.»

If we consider the culture in general, including contemporary art, as a variety of forms of communication — this raises the question how to understand the code, ie, by what means the information can be transmitted, what is the structure of this communication medium, how is it used, how they might be changed, where is their weaknesses and how is it possible to continue the perpetual search for a new artistic language.
